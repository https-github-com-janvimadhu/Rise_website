Chandigarh International Airport (CHIAL) at Mohali

Originally, the all air operations were from the civil enclave of the Indian Air Force (IAF) Station. A new airport building was constructed in the Civil Enclave in April 2011 and was declared a Customs Airport, making it eligible for international flights but no international flight operated from this terminal.


In 2008, the Government of Punjab acquired 304 acres of land on the south side of the existing runway in Jheurheri village at a cost of ₹452 crore to build the new international terminal. A cost which was later shared by Government of Haryana against their equity in CHIAL. CHIAL was established as a Joint Venture between Airports Authority of India (AAI) and Governments of Punjab & Haryana; with AAI a majority stakeholder with 51% equity and the Governments of Punjab & Haryana with an equity share of 24.5% each. The New Terminal Building built at a cost of ₹939 crore was inaugurated by the Prime Minister in September 2015. International flights have started operating from this terminal from 15th September 2016.Air Force Station (AFS) Chandigarh has installed CAT II-B on Runway 29 under the Modernization of Airfield Infrastructure (MAFI) Scheme enabling 24 hour operations with CAT II-B ILS at the airport.
