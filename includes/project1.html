Chandigarh International Airport (CHIAL) at Mohali
  • Originally, the all air operations were from the civil enclave of the Indian Air Force (IAF) Station. A new airport building was constructed in the Civil Enclave in April 2011 and was declared a Customs Airport, making it eligible for international flights but no international flight operated from this terminal.
  • In 2008, the Government of Punjab acquired 304 acres of land on the south side of the existing runway in Jheurheri village at a cost of ₹452 crore to build the new international terminal. A cost which was later shared by Government of Haryana against their equity in CHIAL.
  • CHIAL established as a Joint Venture between Airports Authority of India (AAI) and Governments of Punjab & Haryana; with AAI a majority stakeholder with 51% equity and the Governments of Punjab & Haryana with an equity share of 24.5% each.
  • The New Terminal Building built at a cost of ₹939 crore was inaugurated by the Prime Minister in September 2015. International flights have started operating from this terminal from 15th September 2016.

Recent efforts to enhance to meet the vision of the Department:
  • Runway upgradation:
    • The runway is located in the UT of Chandigarh while the international terminal is Mohali, Punjab. The runway 11/29 had a length of 2743 m (9000 ft). The short runway limited operations of long haul wide-bodied aircraft and only narrow-bodied aircraft of 5000 km radius could operate. 
    • The runway has gone through a major upgrade and repairs. After this, the expanded runway has an effective take-off length of 10,400 ft.
  • 24x7 Operation and Improved Landing Facilities:
    • Upgraded Instrument Landing System (ILS) is being installed to handle aircrafts during low visibility conditions.
    • Air Force Station (AFS) Chandigarh has installed CAT II-B on Runway 29 under the Modernization of Airfield Infrastructure (MAFI) Scheme which will be completed shortly – enabling 24 hour operations with CAT II-B ILS at the airport.
    • CAT III-B ILS which allows aircraft to land with Runway Visual Range (RVR) of up to 50m with a decision height of 15m is also being installed on Runway 11 to enable low visibility landing even during foggy winter months.
  • Air Connectivity:
    • Domestic Flights – There are 34 flights connecting 15 destinations including Delhi, Chennai etc.
    • International Flights – There are 34 flights connecting 15 destinations including Delhi, Chennai etc.
